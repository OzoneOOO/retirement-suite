The Absolute Terminal Value Target
ATV_NOMINAL = Starting Liquid Principal × (1 + CPI_RATE)^30
Do not use Starting Liquid Principal as the target — that is a real figure.

Tier 2 Income Calculation
Recalculate TIEI before computing T2I for every single audit.
Tier 2 Income (T=0 annual) = (Starting Liquid Principal − TIEI) × T2I_RATE
Tax-deferred share of T2: 40%. Remainder taxed as long-term capital gains.
Combined Monthly Income (T=0) = Tier 1 Monthly + (Tier 2 Annual / 12)

Total Initial Equity Investment (TIEI)
TIEI = Down Payment + Transaction Drag (TD)
TD components:
- Statutory Leakage: local transfer tax (confirm rate per city, calculate buyer's statutory share)
- Service Leakage: 1.5% of purchase price
- Acquisition Condition Allowance: 2.0% of purchase price (One-time cash expenditure at T=0. Included in TIEI. Not a recurring cost. Do not apply to maintenance calculations.)

Professional Maintenance Rule
Market-rate labor only. Zero owner labor credit.
Annual Adjusted Maintenance = MAX(Purchase Price × 0.003, (Purchase Price × 0.0175) − Annual HOA)
Maintenance grows at CPI_RATE annually. The 0.3% floor does not. HOA offsets the maintenance the owner would otherwise absorb — do not double-count.

Fiscal Drag (Calculate annually at T=0 values. Grows at CPI_RATE)
- State income tax on Tier 1 pension and Tier 2 LTCG distribution
- Local income or payroll tax where applicable
- Sales tax: confirmed state/local rate × CAME × 12 annually
Do not model SALT deduction cap unless user specifies itemizing.

SECTION II: THE SURPLUS ENGINE
Step 1: Apply Numbeo Cost of Living Index to BMEB to produce city-adjusted monthly expense (CAME). If index = 108, CAME = $2,300 × 1.08. If Numbeo data absent, use BMEB and note caveat. CAME grows at (CPI_RATE-0.5) annually.
Step 2: Compute monthly housing cost (P&I, Net Property Tax/12, HOA, Annual Adjusted Maintenance/12). Net Property Tax = confirmed local rate × OPA/assessed value, less any applicable homestead or primary residence exemption. Do not apply exemptions to TIEI.
Step 3: Vehicle cost: $0/month default ONLY if Walk Score >= 90 and Transit Score >= 70 are confirmed.
Step 4: Monthly fiscal drag = Annual Fiscal Drag / 12.
Step 5: Define the two surplus components:
GROWING_MONTHLY_0 = Combined Monthly Income (T=0) − CAME_0 − [monthly housing costs excluding P&I] − [monthly fiscal drag]
FIXED_MONTHLY = Monthly P&I payment (fixed nominal for loan term; $0 if cash purchase)
Year 0 Monthly Surplus = GROWING_MONTHLY_0 − FIXED_MONTHLY

Step 6: 30-Year Future Value of Surplus Stream
GROWING_ANNUAL_0 = GROWING_MONTHLY_0 × 12
FIXED_ANNUAL = FIXED_MONTHLY × 12
FV_Growing = [GROWING_ANNUAL_0 × (1 + CPI_RATE) × ((1 + NOMINAL_RETURN_RATE)^30 − (1 + CPI_RATE)^30) / (NOMINAL_RETURN_RATE − CPI_RATE)]
FV_Fixed = [FIXED_ANNUAL × ((1 + NOMINAL_RETURN_RATE)^30 − 1) / NOMINAL_RETURN_RATE]
FV_SURPLUS = FV_Growing - FV_Fixed

Step 7: Cashflow-Neutral Down Payment Solve.

Given the audit's purchase price (PP), Starting Liquid Principal, Tier 1 income,
and all carrying-cost variables, solve for the down payment percentage `dp*` that
produces Year 0 Monthly Surplus = 0.

Procedure:
- Both GROWING_MONTHLY and FIXED_MONTHLY are linear in dp.
- GROWING_MONTHLY decreases as dp rises (TIEI grows, capital available for T2
  shrinks, T2I falls).
- FIXED_MONTHLY decreases as dp rises (loan balance shrinks linearly).
- Set GROWING_MONTHLY(dp*) = FIXED_MONTHLY(dp*) and solve algebraically.

Report two consumption metrics:
- Down Payment $ at dp*  =  dp* × PP
- TIEI at dp*  =  (dp* + Statutory_Leakage_Rate + Service_Leakage_Rate +
                  Acquisition_Condition_Allowance_Rate) × PP
- % of Liquid Wealth Consumed (TIEI basis)  =  TIEI(dp*) / Starting Liquid Principal
- % of Liquid Wealth Consumed (DP basis)    =  Down Payment $(dp*) / Starting Liquid Principal

Edge cases:
- If Year 0 Monthly Surplus is already ≥ 0 at the protocol default 20% down,
  report: "Already cashflow-neutral or positive at 20% default — metric N/A."
  Do not report a sub-20% value.

- If Year 0 Monthly Surplus remains < 0 at 100% cash purchase (dp = 1.0),
  no neutral solution exists. Report: "No cashflow-neutral down payment exists
  — carrying costs structurally exceed net income." Flag for Hard Failure
  consideration in zone classification.

- If dp* is feasible mathematically (between 0.20 and 1.00) but TIEI(dp*)
  exceeds Starting Liquid Principal, report dp* with a flag:
  "Solution exceeds available liquid capital — infeasible without external
  capital." This is a soft Hard Failure for this purchase profile.

SECTION III: TERMINAL WEALTH FORMULA
Note that the portfolio component uses only CPI_RATE, not NOMINAL_RETURN_RATE at all. This means the starting portfolio will not grow. This is intentional. The reinvested monthly surplus will grow to compensate for modeling.
Terminal Nominal Wealth = [Home Value at T30 − Remaining Mortgage Debt] + [(Starting Liquid Principal − TIEI) × (1 + CPI_RATE)^30] + FV_SURPLUS
Where: Home Value at T30 = Purchase Price × (1 + local appreciation rate)^30.
Compare Terminal Nominal Wealth to ATV_NOMINAL to find Wealth Delta.
Wealth Delta Index = Wealth Delta / (Combined Monthly Income T=0 × 12).
Year 15 Buffer = [GROWING_MONTHLY_0 × (1 + CPI_RATE)^15 − FIXED_MONTHLY] / [Combined Monthly Income (T=0) × (1 + CPI_RATE)^15]

SECTION IV: CASHFLOW COMFORT ZONES
Compounding Zone: Monthly surplus is strongly positive. Liquid principal compounds aggressively.
Balanced Zone: Monthly surplus is modestly positive or negative. Terminal Nominal Wealth is well above ATV_NOMINAL.
Lifestyle Zone: Monthly surplus is negative but Terminal Nominal Wealth is still above ATV_NOMINAL.
Hard Failure Zone: Monthly surplus is structurally negative at all down payment levels and Terminal Nominal Wealth falls below ATV_NOMINAL.
